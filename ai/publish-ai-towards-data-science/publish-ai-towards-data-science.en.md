## **What works best on TDS**

The articles that resonate most with our audience share a few things in common:

* **Specificity over generality:** “How I Built a Reliable Multi-Agent Pipeline with Claude and OpenClaw” will always outperform “An Introduction to Agentic AI.” Our readers want to learn from your particular experience.
* **Genuine technical depth:** Show the code, explain the trade-offs, and discuss what didn’t work. Practitioners can spot surface-level content immediately, and they’ll scroll right past it.
* **A timely angle:** Tie your piece to something current: a new model capability, a shift in how agents are being architected, a hard-won lesson from a recent project. Give readers a reason to care right now.
* **Your own visuals.** A clear architecture diagram or a well-labeled chart can turn a good article into a shareable one.

Here’s what to expect when you work with us:

* **Word count:** Articles typically range from 1,000 to 5,000 words; long enough to go deep, short enough to respect your reader’s time.
