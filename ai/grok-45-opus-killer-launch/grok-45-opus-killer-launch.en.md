## Cursor deal fuels training data

The model also reportedly received specialized supplemental training data from Cursor, the AI coding platform that SpaceX is in the midst of acquiring as part of a massive $60 billion deal. That could give Grok 4.5 an advantage on software engineering tasks by exposing it to high-quality coding workflows and developer interactions. Musk also teased that an even larger 2-trillion-parameter V-series model is due for an August release.

## Grok 4.5’s trillion-parameter engine

Not surprisingly, Musk referenced “Opus-class” based on the current success of competitor Anthropic’s Claude Opus, which is currently on version 4.8. This is especially fitting timing given the recent drama surrounding Anthropic’s flagship model, Fable 5. Following a weeks-long standoff in which the federal administration imposed [strict export controls on Fable 5](https://thenewstack.io/us-gov-orders-anthropic-to-pull-fable-5-and-mythos-5-three-days-after-launch/) over “jailbreak” national security concerns, Anthropic briefly pulled the model before [redeploying it with stricter safety classifiers](https://thenewstack.io/anthropic-fable-ban-lifted/). However, because Fable 5 now defaults back to Opus 4.8 whenever a security guardrail is tripped — and with access to Fable 5 tightening up — Musk must smell blood in the water.
